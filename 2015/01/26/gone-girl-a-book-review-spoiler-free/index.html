Gone Girl - A Book Review (Spoiler-free!)

Monday, January 26, 2015
Posted in books, gone girl, gillian flynn, review

Thoughts Before I Started Reading

I had heard about this book for a year or two. I heard that it was intense, that the ending wasn't ideal. After hearing so many people mention it, I still never read it. It was on my list, but I kept skipping over it. Then, I watched the movie. They were right - it was intense, and the ending made me angry. I had also heard that the movie ending was different, dramatically. I had to find out for myself.

My Favorite Part

I don't have a favorite moment in this book. My favorite part was the writing itself. It captivated me from the very beginning. The best sentence in the entire book:

My eyes flipped open at exactly six A.M. This was no avian fluttering of the lashes, no gentle blink toward consciousness. The awakening was mechanical. A spooky ventriloquist-dummy click of the lids: The world is black and then, showtime! 6-0-0 the clock said -in my face, first thing I saw. 6-0-0. It felt different. I rarely woke at such a rounded time. I was a man of jagged risings: 8:43, 11:51, 9:26. My life was alarmless.

That's on page 3. The rest is just as good, if not better.

When I Got Bored

I don't know if I ever got bored, but I had to trudge through a few parts that were identical to the movie. Most of the book had bits and pieces that were removed for filming purposes, and I liked that.

Thoughts After I Finished Reading

Gillian Flynn is a fantastic author. I know it's very early but this could be the best book I read in 2015. The suspense was taken away due to watching the movie beforehand, but it was still great.

My Recommendation

Read it. Do yourself a favor and read it before you watch the movie. Trust me on this, your brain will thank me later.

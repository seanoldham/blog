All the Light We Cannot See - A Book Review (Spoiler-free!)

Monday, May 11, 2015
Posted in books, review, all the light we cannot see, anthony doerr

Thoughts Before I Started Reading

Pulitzer Prize-winning books will always attract attention, and this one is no exception. I've had this book on my reading list for a few months now and our book club chose it for the month of May.

My Favorite Part

I liked Werner, but Marie-Laure won my heart pretty early on. Great descriptive writing in regards to the unknown world of a child. She never let the fact that she was blind become a disability, and had more questions about what can't be seen than the things her blindness prevented her from viewing.

When I Got Bored

I can't honestly say that I ever got bored. Fantastic read.

Thoughts After I Finished Reading

It's not exactly a page-turner, but I still couldn't stop. Very descriptive and intriguing.

My Recommendation

This book was chosen on May 2nd and I finished it on the 8th. That should tell you something about the greatness of this story. Historical fiction from a different angle. Read it.

Fixing Family Sharing's App Store & Apple Music Issues

Thursday, July 16, 2015
Posted in apple, bug, icloud

With the recent rollout of Apple Music, my fiancé and I decided it was better (and cheaper) to set up iCloud Family Sharing. Setting it up was simple, no problem there. We're both enjoying Apple Music, everything is great! Well, not so much.

Ever since she became part of the Family, she can't save music or download/update apps.

Our initial thought was that maybe I forgot to give her the correct authority within Family Sharing. Nope, she was listed as an Adult and making her a Parent/Guardian doesn't fix the problem. Our next piece to troubleshoot was the network. I tried downloading from both Wi-Fi and cellular with no good results.

Here's where I tried something different and it actually worked! The first thing I did was log her out of the App Store. I'm sure doing it from the Settings menu would work just fine but I did it from the app itself. The option to log out in the app is at the very bottom of the Featured tab. Don't log out of iCloud in the Settings menu! You could lose your data!

Once she was signed out, I reset the phone. I did the hard reset (hold home & power buttons down until the screen turns off) but a normal power down should do the trick.

When the phone was back on and ready, we logged her back into iCloud and everything worked as it should!

My theory is that iCloud saw her join a Family Sharing group and it freaked out and broke something. A complete reset from the App Store let her account once again authenticate correctly and now everything is working as it should.

This shouldn't be necessary, it's a bug that Apple can fix without much of an update. However, it was a problem for us and it could be a problem for other people. I hope this helps anyone that is having this issue. If you have questions or comments for me, find me on Twitter (the link is at the top of this page).

My Data Privacy Twitter Rant

Thursday, November 06, 2014

Roughly 230 people follow me on Twitter. Those people just read (or will soon) a mini rant regarding data privacy. I feel quite strongly about data privacy, and it frustrates me to see and hear people say they're being "violated" by companies that use their data. You gave them that data, what did you expect?!

As much as I appreciate Amazon's latest Prime benefit (Prime Photos), I don't think I want them storing my photos.

— Sean Oldham (@seanoldham) November 6, 2014

"If it's free, you're the product." is something that I really think about these days. Another reason I like iOS over Android. No Google.

— Sean Oldham (@seanoldham) November 6, 2014

Google/Facebook/Dropbox etc use the data you give them through their free service to profit. I'm sure Twitter does too, but not as blatant.

— Sean Oldham (@seanoldham) November 6, 2014

I have no problem paying for cloud storage when I know I'm not the product. I'm buying and paying for the product.

— Sean Oldham (@seanoldham) November 6, 2014

Apple doesn't need my data, they already have my money. Or, maybe I'm just being naive.

— Sean Oldham (@seanoldham) November 6, 2014

I realize there are probably far fewer than 230 people reading my personal blog but if you are, what are your thoughts on data privacy?
